				<h2></h2>
				<p class="tl-ah-about-text">The Million Mom March is a grassroots movement started by Donna Dees-Thomases after the tragic shooting in Granada Hills, California on August 10, 1999. ;xNLx;;xNLx;More than 750,000 people marched in Washington, D.C. on Mother’s Day, May 14th, 2000. An estimated 250,000 marched in satellite rallies in more than 70 different cities around the country.;xNLx;;xNLx;After the march, Million Mom March was reorganized into chapters around the country. In 2001, the organization united with Handgun Control, Inc. and the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence to form the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence and the Brady Center, their chapters continue to advocate for gun violence prevention legislation and social change.;xNLx;;xNLx;[Download and read the full Million Mom March time line](http://www.bradycampaign.org/sites/default/files/Million-Mom-March-Full-Timeline.pdf)</p>
				
				<div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Historic Childproof Handgun Bill Signed Into Law</h3><p>After four years of debate in New Jersey, the childproof handgun bill finally passed both houses of the New Jersey Legislature, and was signed into law with the Million Mom March moms and Ceasefire NJ leaders present. Twelve years later Brady’s lawyers sued the NJ Attorney General’s office to issue the state mandated report on the status of smart gun technology.</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Million Mom March Founded</h3><p>In Granada Hills, California, a white supremacist stormed a day camp and fired 70 shots, wounding five people including three children.   Outraged, Donna Dees-Thomases, a Short Hills, New Jersey mom and part-time comedy publicist, applied for a permit to march on Washington on Mother’s Day, 2000, calling it The Million Mom March.  She set out to mobilize moms in all 435 congressional districts over the next 9 months with the rallying cry: "Looking for a Few Good Moms to Mobilize for Common Sense Gun Laws."</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>MMM's Youngest Organizer Promotes her Upcoming Appearance on the Rosie O'Donnell Show</h3><p>First grader and activist Sophie embarks on a whirlwind publicity tour on local Missouri television to promote her upcoming appearance on The Rosie O’Donnell Show. After watching Sophie’s guest appearance, thousands of supporters signed up across the nation. This would be the first of numerous appearances on Rosie’s show to promote the Million Mom March.</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Million Mom March Held in Washington, DC</h3><p>On Mother's Day in 2000, 750,000 moms and advocates marched on the National Mall to call for common sense regulations on firearms in the United States. Satellite marches were held in over 70 cities across the nation, attracting an estimated 250,000 more supporters.</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Gun Reform in New York</h3><p>NY Million Mom March chapters lobbied for passage of sweeping gun reform laws proposed by New Yorkers Against Gun Violence. These reforms were ultimately signed into law by Gov. George Pataki in 2000. </p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>A MMM Leader Becomes National Spokesperson for Asking Saves Kids Campaign</h3><p>A Million Mom March leader from Maryland became National Spokesperson for the ASK (Asking Saves Kids) Campaign. Over next 10-12 years Million Mom March activists bought and distributed more than 3 million ASK brochures to schools around the country.</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Moms March to the Polls, and WIN</h3><p>Sarah Brady, Susan Sarandon and other speakers at Million Mom March 2000 warned lawmakers, "Change the laws or we’ll change the law makers," - and they did. The Million Mom March Voter Education Project surveyed candidates in the 2000 Presidential Election. Moms in Michigan, Washington State and Missouri educated voters on the firearm-related voting records of the incumbent U.S. senators from those states. All three were defeated. Million Mom March voters chose Florida candidate Bill Nelson, a gun sense advocate, as US Senator over Bill McCollum. Million Mom March members also worked with regional coalitions to help pass the Oregon and Colorado Referendums, closing the gun show loopholes in those states.</p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>One Year Anniversary of Million Mom March</h3><p>Million Mom March chapters celebrated the one year anniversary of the historic march with dozens of rallies across the nation. Death threats on organizers forced a large rally in Albany to be cancelled. Despite the setback, this hardship helped strengthen the resolve of the activists. Newly elected U.S. Senator Hillary Clinton joined New York Governor George Pataki for an indoor MMM rally in White Plains. </p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Legislative Victories in Texas</h3><p>MMM Texas worked with grassroots organizations Texans Against Gun Violence, Texans for Gun Safety, Texas Council on Family Violence and SafeRange to secure two great victories in the Texas Legislature. SB 199, a sweeping firearm reform, was signed by Governor Perry. This bill made it illegal for those under final protective orders for family violence and those convicted of family violence crimes to possess firearms. The pet gun lobby bill of the session was also defeated. </p></div><div class="tl-ah-story-summary"><h3>Million Mom March Merges with Handgun Control, Inc. to Become Brady Campaign</h3><p>The Million Mom March merged with Handgun Control, Inc. The new organization was called The Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence. </p></div>			</div>
