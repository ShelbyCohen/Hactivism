        <h1 class="title" id="page-title">Civil Rights, Disability Rights</h1>
            <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
        
  <div class="content clearfix">
    
<article class="node node-content-page node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-mattd odd clearfix" id="node-content-page-38">
      
  <div class="content clearfix">
          <div class='grid-13 alpha omega header-box'>
        <div class='grid-5 alpha'>
          <div class="header-text"><p>The efforts by people with disabilities to participate fully in society began centuries ago and gained momentum in the 20th century.</p>
</div>        </div>
        <div class='grid-8 omega'>
          <div class="header-image"><img src="https://everybody.si.edu/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/images/Civil_Rights_Disability_Rights_header.jpg?itok=HBB5Yr8K" width="398" height="286" alt="black white protesting photograph disability history america" title="“I can’t even get to the back of the bus&quot;; ADAPT activists protesting for accessible transportation, Philadelphia, 1990" /></div>        </div>
      </div>
        
    <div class='grid-11 suffix-2 alpha omega'>
      <div class="body clearfix"><p>Full citizenship entails full inclusion. Citizens successfully challenged legal inequalities around race, culminating in the 1954 Brown v. Board of Education decision by the Supreme Court that struck down legal segregation. In the decades that followed, women, Native Americans, lesbian, bi-sexual, gay and transgender people, Chicanos and Latinos, and other groups pushed to extend the rights of citizenship.</p>
<p>The Disability Rights Movement, organized in the 1970s, has been similar to other movements in important ways. Attitudes needed changing for social change to happen. Paternalism thwarted access to basic rights like education, marriage, work, and housing. Segregation and discrimination contributed to poverty and isolation.</p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.538em;">The Disability Rights Movement has been unlike other movements in important ways, however. For social change to occur, architectural barriers also needed changing. Necessary accommodation and services were different than those for other groups. And because disability crosses race, class, and gender, and people came to it in many different ways, affiliations and expectations also varied.</span></p>
<p>Laura Hershey (1962-2010) was a lifelong disability activist and writer who lived in Denver. This poem captures the importance of advocacy and the motivation behind the work done by disability activists.</p>
</div>      
  <aside class="node node-internal-call-out node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-staceym even clearfix" id="node-internal-call-out-70">
  <header>
    <h2 class="node-title">You Get Proud By Practicing</h2>
  </header>

  <div class="content clearfix">
    <div class="body clearfix"><p><span style="line-height: 1.538em;">by Laura Hershey</span></p>
<p><em>If you are not proud<br />For who you are, for what you say, for how you look;<br />If every time you stop<br />To think of yourself, you do not see yourself glowing<br />With golden light; do not, therefore, give up on yourself.<br />You can get proud.</em></p>
<p><em>You do not need<br />A better body, a purer spirit, or a Ph.D.<br />To be proud.<br />You do not need<br />A lot of money, a handsome boyfriend, or a nice car.<br />You do not need<br />To be able to walk, or see, or hear,<br />Or use big, complicated words,<br />Or do any of those things that you just can’t do<br />To be proud. A caseworker<br />Cannot make you proud,<br />Or a doctor.<br />You only need more practice.<br />You get proud by practicing.</em></p>
<p><em>There are many many ways to get proud.<br />You can try riding a horse, or skiing on one leg,<br />Or playing guitar,<br />And do well or not so well,<br />And be glad you tried<br />Either way.<br />You can show<br />Something you’ve made<br />To someone you respect<br />And be happy with it no matter<br />What they say.<br />You can say<br />What you think, though you know<br />Other people do not think the same way, and you can<br />keep saying it, even if they tell you<br /><em>You are crazy.</em></em></p>
<p><em>You can add your voice<br />All night to the voices<br />Of a hundred and fifty others<br />In a circle<br />Around a jailhouse<br />Where your brothers and sisters are being held<br />For blocking buses with no lifts,<br />Or you can be one of the ones<br />Inside the jailhouse,<br />Knowing of the circle outside.<br />You can speak your love<br />To a friend<br />Without fear.<br />You can find someone who will listen to you<br />Without judging you or doubting you or being<br />Afraid of you<br />And let you hear yourself perhaps<br />For the very first time.<br />These are all ways<br />Of getting proud.<br />None of them<br />Are easy, but all of them<br />Are possible. You can do all of these things,<br />Or just one of them again and again.<br />You get proud<br />By practicing.</em></p>
<p><em>Power makes you proud, and power<br />Comes in many fine forms<br />Supple and rich as butterfly wings.<br />It is music<br />when you practice opening your mouth<br />And liking what you hear<br />Because it is the sound of your own<br />True voice.</em></p>
<p><em>It is sunlight<br />When you practice seeing<br />Strength and beauty in everyone,<br />Including yourself.<br />It is dance<br />when you practice knowing<br />That what you do<br />And the way you do it<br />Is the right way for you<br />And cannot be called wrong.<br />All these hold<br />More power than weapons or money<br />Or lies.<br />All these practices bring power, and power<br />Makes you proud.<br />You get proud<br />By practicing.</em></p>
<p><em>Remember, you weren’t the one<br />Who made you ashamed,<br />But you are the one<br />Who can make you proud.<br />Just practice,<br />Practice until you get proud, and once you are proud,<br />Keep practicing so you won’t forget.<br />You get proud<br />By practicing.</em></p>
</div>  </div>
