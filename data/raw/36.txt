According to <a href="https://www.oromiamedia.org/2016/08/06/omn-oduu-amma-nu-gahe-hag-6-2016/" target="_blank">Oromia media Network</a>, security forces used live bullets against peaceful protestors, killing over <a href="http://www.business-standard.com/article/news-ians/104-killed-in-ethiopia-anti-government-protests-116080900085_1.html" target="_blank">100 protestors. </a></div><div class="zn-body__paragraph"><h3>Annexation</h3></div><div class="zn-body__read-more"><div class="read-more-gradient"></div><div class="read-more-button"><div class="read-more-link" id="js-body-read-more">Read More</div></div></div><div class="zn-body__read-all"><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Oromos have been <a href="https://www.amnesty.org/en/documents/afr25/006/2014/en/" target="_blank">staging protest rallies</a> across the country since April of 2014 against systematic marginalization and persecution of ethnic Oromos. The immediate trigger of the protest was a development plan that sought to expand the territorial limits of Ethiopia's capital, Addis Ababa, into neighbouring Oromo villages and towns. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph"><h3>US Influence</h3></div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The United States see the Ethiopian government as a critical partner on the Global War on Terror. </div><div Obama doubled down on previous endorsements by administration officials by describing the government as 'democratically-elected."</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph"><h3>A police state </h3></div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">However, consistent reports by the US government itself and other human rights organizations depict an image of a police state whose apparatus of surveillance and control permeates the entire society down to household levels. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The US led 'war on terror', started by President George Bush, provided the government with a political and legal instrument with which the government justified severe restrictions on freedom of expression, assembly, and association. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The 2009 Anti-Terrorism Proclamation, one of the most draconian pieces of anti-terrorism legislations in the world, enabled the government to stretch its power of prosecution and punishment beyond what is permissible under standard criminal and constitutional law rules.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">In recent years, terrorism trials have become the most significant legal instrument frequently used by the authorities to secure and consolidate the prevailing relationship of power between the ruling ethnic Tigrayan elites and other ethnic groups in the country. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Under the pretext of 'fighting terrorism', the regime exiled, prosecuted and convicted several opposition leaders, community leaders, journalists, bloggers, and activists; paralyzing criticisms of any type. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">In its 2015 report titled Ethiopia's Anti-Terrorism Law: A Tool to Stifle Dissent, the Oakland Institute details the ways in which Ethiopian authorities systematically appropriate the anti-terrorism law to annihilate dissent and opposition to the policies of the ruling party. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph"><h3>Denial</h3></div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">As of July, the protests have been spreading into the Amhara region, home to the second largest ethnic group in the country.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The Amharas and Oromos, which constitute well over two-third of the country's population, are seen as 'historical antagonists'. The ruling party transformed this antagonism between the two ethnic groups into a productive political tool.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">According to the governing narrative, Oromos are only political force that can rescue Ethiopia from both threats.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">These governing narratives are being exposed as the two groups begun to see how these narratives were crafted and are expressing solidarity towards each other as victims of the same system. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The Ethiopian government is in denial and making the same promises of
