<p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; text-align: center; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal" align="center">
<span style="font-size: 16pt; font-family: Arial">Health At Every Size<sup>&reg;</sup> Fact Sheet</span>
</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; text-align: center; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal" align="center">
<strong style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The Health At Every Size<sup>&reg;</sup> (HAES<sup>&reg;</sup>)
approach is an alternative to the <br />
weight/size-based paradigm upon which much
current public health policy is based.&nbsp; </strong><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black"><br />
<br />
The Health At Every Size&reg; principles are:</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Accepting and respecting the diversity
of body shapes and sizes</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Recognizing that health and well-being
are multi-dimensional and that they <br />
&nbsp;include physical, social, spiritual,
occupational, emotional, and intellectual aspects</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0.25in; text-align: justify; text-indent: -0.25in; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Promoting all aspects of health and
well-being for people of all sizes </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Promoting eating in a manner which
balances individual nutritional needs, hunger,<br />
&nbsp;satiety, appetite, and pleasure </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Promoting individually appropriate,
enjoyable, life-enhancing physical activity, rather <br />
&nbsp;than exercise that is focused
on a goal of weight loss<sup>1</sup> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">There is considerable
scientific evidence supporting the </strong><strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">HAES&reg; approach and
<br />
establishing that &ldquo;obesity&rdquo; is not the health risk it has been reported to be.</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Weight and BMI are poor predictors of
disease and longevity.<span class="smallText"><sup>2,3,4,5,6</sup></span> The <br />
&nbsp; bulk of epidemiological evidence
suggests that five pounds &ldquo;underweight&rdquo; is<br />
&nbsp; more dangerous than 75 pounds
&ldquo;overweight.&rdquo;<span class="smallText"><sup>4,7</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Multiple studies are suggesting that a
focus on weight as a health criterion is <br />
&nbsp;often misdirected and harmful.<span class="smallText"><sup>8,9,10</sup></span>
</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">In a study comparing the HAES&reg; model to
a diet approach, though only dieters<br />
&nbsp;lost weight, <em>both</em> groups initially had similar improvements in metabolic
fitness, <br />
&nbsp;activity levels, psychological measures, and eating behaviors.&nbsp; After two years, <br />
&nbsp;dieters had <em>regained</em> their weight and <em>lost</em> the health improvements, while the <br />
&nbsp;HAES&reg;
group <em>sustained</em> their health
improvements.<span class="smallText"><sup>11</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The HAES&reg; approach is
based on substantial data which documents that weight <br />
loss programs are not
effective at improving health and often cause harm.</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Restrictive dieting is an ineffective
long-term prescription for &ldquo;obesity,&rdquo; as up to 95% of <br />
&nbsp;dieters regain the weight
they lost, and sometimes more, within three years.<span class="smallText"><sup>12,13</sup>&nbsp;</span> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Restrictive dieting and weight cycling
can lead to physical complications including <br />
&nbsp;slowed metabolism, reduced muscle
tissue and body temperature,<br />
&nbsp;and eating disorders.<span class="smallText"><sup>14,15</sup></span> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Weight-loss surgery (WLS) intentionally
damages healthy organs in order to force <br />
&nbsp;adherence to a restrictive diet and
incurs a host of short- and long-term risks <br />
&nbsp;including death and malnutrition.<span class="smallText"><sup>16,17</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The HAES&reg; approach focuses on the empirically-validated
factors that are associated <br />
with health and longevity, for people across the
weight spectrum. The HAES&reg; approach does not waste money.</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Using BMI as a proxy for health,
traditional approaches misidentify those who<br />
&nbsp;need intervention. </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">A recent government survey indicated
that over half the &ldquo;overweight&rdquo; adults <br />
&nbsp;(51.3%) being targeted are metabolically
healthy, and one in four &ldquo;normal weight&rdquo; <br />
&nbsp;(23.5%) metabolically unhealthy adults
are overlooked.<span class="smallText"><sup>18</sup></span> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Therefore, even assuming weight loss
were possible, and that it worked to improve<br />
&nbsp; health, half of every dollar is
wasted and one in five people who need help do not get it.</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The HAES&reg; approach provides an alternative which avoids the
harmful consequences of efforts to combat &ldquo;childhood obesity&rdquo; and the denial of equal access to health care
coverage and service to millions of adults, both of which create additional
health risks for these individuals.</strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp; Singling out larger
children and youth for weight-related interventions in schools <br />
increases both
anxiety for the child and stigmatization, prejudice, and harassment towards the
child.<span class="smallText"><sup>19,20,21</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">81% of 10-year-olds admit to dieting,
binge eating, or a fear of getting fat and we are now <br />
seeing eating disorders
in children as young as five.<span class="smallText"><sup>22</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Insurers deny health care coverage
simply because of high BMI, independent of overall <br />
health, despite research
showing that weight cannot be directly correlated to health status.<span class="smallText"><sup>18</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 9.35pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -9.35pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">Routine preventative health services
are denied to people who are of high BMI.<span class="smallText"><sup>23,24</sup></span> Many <br />
larger
individuals avoid seeking health care due to stigma and shame.<span class="smallText"><sup>23,24</sup></span></span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The HAES&reg; approach does not add to the stigma against people
of size. </strong>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 11pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -11pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">One of the major vectors for weight
stigma is the well-documented bias against fat people among healthcare
providers, policy makers, and those in the weight loss industry.<span class="smallText"><sup>25,26,27</sup></span>
</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 11pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -11pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The stress of experiencing stigma and
discrimination is also believed to account for health disparities seen in minority
groups, including individuals of size.<span class="smallText"><sup>28</sup></span> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 0in; text-align: justify; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<strong style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">In health care policy and service provision the implementation of
the <br />
evidence-based HAES&reg; paradigm will refocus efforts on health practices for
people of all sizes and improve health without perpetuating stigma.</strong><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black"> </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 11pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -11pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">The HAES&reg; model will ensure that individuals of all sizes are
allowed access<br />
to healthcare coverage and to quality affordable health care
prevention and treatment services. </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 11pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -11pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">In education and employment, the HAES&reg; model will provide that <em>all</em> youth and adults have opportunities
to learn the importance of eating nutrient rich foods and engaging in enjoyable
physical activity from a weight-neutral perspective. </span>
</p>
<p style="margin: 0in 2.15pt 3pt 11pt; text-align: justify; text-indent: -11pt; line-height: normal" class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">&bull;&nbsp; </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial; color: black">In diversity training programs in employment, policy, and school
environments the HAES&reg; model ensures that size-diversity is included in the
human diversities to be valued and respected.&nbsp;
</span>
</p>
