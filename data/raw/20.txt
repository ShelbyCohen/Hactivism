   <p>Catalina Maria Johnson, the host and producer of <em><a href="http://beatlatino.com/" target="_blank">Beat Latino</a></em>, visits <em>World Cafe</em> to discuss the music associated with the Chicano Power movement of the 1960s, including songs by activist and folk singer <a href="http://www.npr.org/artists/482177523/agust-n-lira" target="_blank">Agustn Lira</a>. In some ways, she says, this music has become the oral history of the time.</p>   <p>Hear a song by Lira, plus examples of present day Chicano protest music, in Johnson's <a href="https://open.spotify.com/user/catalinamariajohnson/playlist/2cdcqrPQG6T9XTRLEZt2Xr">Spotify playlist</a>.</p>   <div id="res491375269" class="bucketwrap statichtml">
