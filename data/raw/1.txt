<h1 class="headerline">Definition: What is an ACE?</h1>

<h2><em>An adverse childhood experience (ACE) describes a traumatic experience in a person&rsquo;s life occurring before the age of 18 that the person remembers as an adult.
</em></h2>

<p>
In a 2011 Minnesota telephone survey, individuals were asked if they had experienced 
any of nine types of ACEs.  The nine ACEs are:
	<ul>
		<li>physical abuse</li>
		<li>sexual abuse</li>
		<li>emotional abuse</li>
		<li>mental illness of a household member</li>
		<li>problematic drinking or alcoholism of a household member</li>
		<li>illegal street or prescription drug use by a household member</li>
		<li>divorce or separation of a parent</li>
		<li>domestic violence towards a parent</li>
		<li>incarceration of a household member</li>
	</ul>
</p>

<p>The ACE score is a measure of cumulative exposure to adverse childhood conditions. Exposure to any single ACE condition is counted as one point.  If a person experienced none of the conditions in childhood, the ACE score is zero.  Points are then totaled for a final ACE score.  It is important to note that the ACE score does not capture the frequency or severity of any given ACE in a person&rsquo;s life, focusing instead on the number of ACE conditions experienced.  In addition, the ACE conditions used in the ACE survey reflect only a select list of experiences.</p>
