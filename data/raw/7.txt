<p><b>Anti-nuclear organizations</b> may oppose <a href="/wiki/Uranium_mining" title="Uranium mining">uranium mining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a>, and/or <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a>. Anti-nuclear groups have undertaken public <a href="/wiki/Protest" title="Protest">protests</a> and acts of <a href="/wiki/Civil_disobedience" title="Civil disobedience">civil disobedience</a> which have included occupations of nuclear plant sites. Some of the most influential groups in the anti-nuclear movement have had members who were elite scientists, including several Nobel Laureates and many nuclear physicists.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_organizations">Types of organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-nuclear_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types of organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Various types of organizations have identified themselves with the <a href="/wiki/Anti-nuclear_movement" title="Anti-nuclear movement">anti-nuclear movement</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Direct_action" title="Direct action">direct action</a> groups, such as the <a href="/wiki/Clamshell_Alliance" title="Clamshell Alliance">Clamshell Alliance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shad_Alliance" title="Shad Alliance">Shad Alliance</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> groups, such as <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Consumer_protection" title="Consumer protection">consumer protection</a> groups, such as <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Nader" title="Ralph Nader">Ralph Nader</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Critical_Mass_(Anti-nuclear_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Critical Mass (Anti-nuclear group)">Critical Mass</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professional</a> organizations,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> such as <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Concerned_Scientists" title="Union of Concerned Scientists">Union of Concerned Scientists</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_Physicians_for_the_Prevention_of_Nuclear_War" title="International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War">International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War</a>; and</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> such as <a href="/wiki/European_Free_Alliance" title="European Free Alliance">European Free Alliance</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Some of the most influential groups in the anti-nuclear movement have had members who were elite scientists, including several Nobel Laureates and many nuclear physicists. In the United States, these scientists have belonged primarily to three groups: the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Concerned_Scientists" title="Union of Concerned Scientists">Union of Concerned Scientists</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_American_Scientists" title="Federation of American Scientists">Federation of American Scientists</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Committee_for_Nuclear_Responsibility" title="Committee for Nuclear Responsibility">Committee for Nuclear Responsibility</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-nuclear_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anti-nuclear groups have undertaken public <a href="/wiki/Protest" title="Protest">protests</a> and acts of <a href="/wiki/Civil_disobedience" title="Civil disobedience">civil disobedience</a> which have included occupations of nuclear plant sites. Other salient strategies have included lobbying, petitioning government authorities, influencing <a href="/wiki/Public_policy" title="Public policy">public policy</a> through referendum campaigns and involvement in elections. Anti-nuclear groups have also tried to influence policy implementation through litigation and by participating in licensing proceedings.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_organizations">International organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-nuclear_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_ATOM_Project" title="The ATOM Project">The ATOM Project</a>, an International nonprofit organization seeking entry into force of the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty and the limitation of all nuclear arsenals.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/European_Nuclear_Disarmament" title="European Nuclear Disarmament">European Nuclear Disarmament</a>, which held annual conventions in the 1980s involving thousands of anti-nuclear weapons activists mostly from Western Europe but also from Eastern Europe, the United States, and Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-Wit164_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit164-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Friends of the Earth International">Friends of the Earth International</a>, a network of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_organization" title="Environmental organization">environmental organizations</a> in 77 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Global_Zero" title="Global Zero">Global Zero</a>, an international non-partisan group of 300 <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_leader&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World leader (page does not exist)">world leaders</a> dedicated to achieving the elimination of nuclear weapons.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Global_Initiative_to_Combat_Nuclear_Terrorism" title="Global Initiative to Combat Nuclear Terrorism">Global Initiative to Combat Nuclear Terrorism</a>, an international partnership of 83 nations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Greenpeace_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenpeace International">Greenpeace International</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">non-governmental</a> <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> organization<sup id="cite_ref-UN_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN-9">[9]</a></sup> with offices in over 41 countries and headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GPI_world_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPI_world-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Campaign_to_Abolish_Nuclear_Weapons" title="International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons">International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Network_of_Engineers_and_Scientists_for_Global_Responsibility" title="International Network of Engineers and Scientists for Global Responsibility">International Network of Engineers and Scientists for Global Responsibility</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Physicians_for_the_Prevention_of_Nuclear_War" title="International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War">International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War</a>, which had affiliates in 41 nations in 1985, representing 135,000 physicians;<sup id="cite_ref-Wit164_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit164-6">[6]</a></sup> IPPNW was awarded the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/UNESCO_Prize_for_Peace_Education" title="UNESCO Prize for Peace Education">Peace Education Prize</a> in 1984 and the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Free_World_Policy" title="Nuclear Free World Policy">Nuclear Free World Policy</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Information_and_Resource_Service" title="Nuclear Information and Resource Service">Nuclear Information and Resource Service</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/OPANAL" title="OPANAL">OPANAL</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Parliamentarians_for_Nuclear_Non-Proliferation_and_Disarmament" title="Parliamentarians for Nuclear Non-Proliferation and Disarmament">Parliamentarians for Nuclear Non-Proliferation and Disarmament</a>, a global network of over 700 parliamentarians from more than 75 countries working to prevent nuclear proliferation.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pax_Christi_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Pax Christi International">Pax Christi International</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> group which took a "sharply anti-nuclear stand".<sup id="cite_ref-Wit164_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit164-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ploughshares_Fund" title="Ploughshares Fund">Ploughshares Fund</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pugwash_Conferences_on_Science_and_World_Affairs" title="Pugwash Conferences on Science and World Affairs">Pugwash Conferences on Science and World Affairs</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Socialist_International" title="Socialist International">Socialist International</a>, the world body of social democratic parties.<sup id="cite_ref-Wit128_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit128-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dka_Gakkai" class="mw-redirect" title="Sōka Gakkai">Sōka Gakkai</a>, a peace-orientated <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> organisation, which held anti-nuclear exhibitions in Japanese cities during the late 1970s, and gathered 10 million signatures on petitions calling for the abolition of nuclear weapons.<sup id="cite_ref-Wit128_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit128-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wit125_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit125-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Ribbon_International" title="The Ribbon International">The Ribbon International</a>, a United Nations Non-Governmental Organization promoting <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_disarmament" title="Nuclear disarmament">nuclear disarmament</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_Disarmament_Affairs" title="United Nations Office for Disarmament Affairs">United Nations Office for Disarmament Affairs</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fenner_Brockway,_Baron_Brockway" title="Fenner Brockway, Baron Brockway">World Disarmament Campaign</a><sup id="cite_ref-Wit128_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wit128-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
