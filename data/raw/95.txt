<p><br />
</p><p><b>Movement for a Democratic Society</b> is a multi-issue activist organization affiliated with the newly revived <a href="/Students_for_a_Democratic_Society" title="Students for a Democratic Society">Students for a Democratic Society</a> (SDS). Many of its members are veterans of the New Left in the 60's and 70's. The goal of MDS is the creation of a more egalitarian society in both the political and economic spheres. 
</p>
<dl><dd><i>MDS believes in participatory democracy, the expansion of human rights, universal healthcare, the rejection of discrimination based on race, gender or sexual preference, the preservation of the earth's environment, the expansion of workers' rights, a more equitable distribution of wealth, and the rejection of militarism and war as a way of resolving differences among peoples and nations.</i></dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MDS_Constitution_preamble_2009">MDS Constitution preamble 2009</span></h2>
<p><i>Movement for a Democratic Society is a global association of people on the left. It seeks to create a sustained community of educational and political concern and actions: one bringing together liberals and  radicals, activists and scholars, students, faculty and workers in all trades.  It maintains a vision of a democratic society, where at all levels  the  people have control of the decisions which affect them and the resources on which they are dependent. It seeks a relevance through the continual focus on realities and on the programs necessary to effect change at the most basic levels of economic, political, and social organization. It feels the urgency to put forth a radical, democratic program counter-posed to authoritarian movements<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>.</i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>In early 2006, a group of former <a href="/Students_for_a_Democratic_Society" title="Students for a Democratic Society">Students for a Democratic  Society</a> members and sympathisers, led by <a href="/Democratic_Socialists_of_America" title="Democratic Socialists of America">Democratic Socialists of America</a> activist <a href="/Paul_Buhle" title="Paul Buhle">Paul Buhle</a> joined with some a new generation of college students to re-found <a href="/Students_for_a_Democratic_Society" title="Students for a Democratic Society">Students for a Democratic Society</a>. The movement now has over 130 chapters across the USA.
</p><p>Also  Buhle and others founded an SDS support group for older activists-<strong class="selflink">Movement for a Democratic Society</strong> .
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MDS_founders.2FWeatherman_controvesy">MDS founders/Weatherman controvesy</span></h2>
<p><a href="/Paul_Buhle" title="Paul Buhle">Paul Buhle</a>, <a href="/Thomas_Good" title="Thomas Good">Thomas Good</a> and <a href="/Bruce_Rubenstein" title="Bruce Rubenstein">Bruce Rubenstein</a> played key roles in founding the <strong class="selflink">Movement for a Democratic Society</strong> in 2006.
</p><p>According to an article on <strong class="selflink">Movement for a Democratic Society</strong> and the  new <a href="/Students_for_a_Democratic_Society" title="Students for a Democratic Society">Students for a Democratic Society</a>  in <a href="/The_Nation" title="The Nation">The Nation</a> April 2007;
</p>
<dl><dd><i>Power has resided largely with three figures: historian Paul Buhle, once editor of the original SDS journal Radical America; Thomas Good, a 48-year-old Communist-turned-anarchist who created the new SDS website and Bruce Rubenstein, a Connecticut personal-injury attorney.</i></dd></dl>
<p><a href="/The_Nation" title="The Nation">The Nation</a> went  on to comment on tensions within MDS caused by the dominance of the Weatherman clique;
</p><p>The Weather controversy blew up  when former <a href="/Weather_Underground" title="Weather Underground" class="mw-redirect">Weather Underground</a> leader <a href="/Bernardine_Dohrn" title="Bernardine Dohrn">Bernardine Dohrn</a>, was invited to speak at the first new SDS conference, held in Providence, Rhode Island, in April 2006. Dohrn received a rousing welcome, but when <a href="/Bob_Ross" title="Bob Ross">Bob Ross</a>, an early SDS leader, used his talk to lament that "the largest legal and unarmed movement in the history of the West" turned "ineffectually violent and useless," he was received coolly.
</p><p>At the first new SDS national convention in Chicago, in August, Good opened the proceedings by reading greetings from Dohrn. Moreover, Bruce Rubenstein, MDS's treasurer, was unapologetic about his Weather history and says that if it were 1969 he would "do it all over again...<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>"
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MDS.2C_Incorporated">MDS, Incorporated</span></h2>
<p>"The derision and disrespect for criticisms (going back to Banning Brigade disregard of democratic decisions made for the 2006 SDS Convention and the controversy which arose following the 2006 convention's decision to approve the formation of MDS, Inc.) which the Banning Brigade has dished out, and the crude insults they introduced into our sds/mds conversation have predictably resulted in more of the same, escalation of anger and frustration."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>"In February [2007] MDS held a daylong public meeting to announce a nonprofit corporation, MDS, Inc., that will raise funds for <a href="/Students_for_a_Democratic_Society" title="Students for a Democratic Society">SDS</a>. One of the selected speakers, former <a href="/Weather_Underground_Organization" title="Weather Underground Organization">Weather</a> leader <a href="/Mark_Rudd" title="Mark Rudd">Mark Rudd</a>, delivered a piercingly honest self-criticism, stating that Weather “did the work” of the FBI by “killing off” the original SDS. Rudd urged the new SDS to recognize violence and property destruction as politically self-defeating in the United States...
</p><p>"The newly elected board of MDS, Inc., is broadly representative of the whole left, but its biggest names–<a href="/Tom_Hayden" title="Tom Hayden">Tom Hayden</a>, <a href="/Barbara_Ehrenreich" title="Barbara Ehrenreich">Barbara Ehrenreich</a>, <a href="/Cornel_West" title="Cornel West">Cornel West</a>, <a href="/Angela_Davis" title="Angela Davis">Angela Davis</a> and <a href="/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a>–are symbolic luminaries, not actively involved. Much hinges on whether the new chairman, <a href="/Manning_Marable" title="Manning Marable">Manning Marable</a>, a distinguished African-American historian at Columbia University, can guide MDS, Inc., beyond its present contretemps to “assist and promote the development of activism among young people,” as he envisions."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
