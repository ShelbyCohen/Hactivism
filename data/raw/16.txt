      </p></section><div id="article-body" class="article-body"><h2><span lang="EN-GB">What is BDS?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">BDS stands for Boycott Divestment and Sanctions (and not, as Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has </span>claimed<span lang="EN-GB">, for "bigotry, dishonesty and shame").</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">The BDS movement's three aims are grounded in international law and fundamental rights. </span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">It seeks to end the occupation and dismantle Israel's illegal wall and settlements, demands full equality for Palestinian citizens of Israel, and calls for the rights of Palestinian refugees to be upheld.</span> </p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">When did it start and why?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">The BDS movement began when a coalition of 170 Palestinian civil society groups issued a </span>call<span lang="EN-GB"> to "people of conscience" around the world on July 9, 2005.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Palestinians </span>argue<span lang="EN-GB"> that a global citizens' movement is necessary because - despite decades of "</span>peace process" facade<span lang="EN-GB"> - political leaders have failed to end Israel's settler-colonialism, ethnic cleansing and apartheid practices. In fact, they continue to enable them.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Therefore, only bottom-up pressure from ordinary people will force governments to end Israel's impunity and help create a just peace based on </span>freedom, justice and equality<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">What has the BDS movement achieved?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">In the decade since its launch, the BDS movement has gradually accumulated successes around the world, from </span>US churches<span lang="EN-GB"> to </span>UK campuses<span lang="EN-GB">, </span>Egyptian trade unions<span lang="EN-GB"> to the </span>Bolivian government<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">In the </span>economic<span lang="EN-GB"> sphere, </span>Veolia<span lang="EN-GB"> and </span>G4S<span lang="EN-GB"> - multinational corporations involved in Israel's oppression of Palestinians - lost billions of dollars due to BDS campaigns and announced withdrawals from Israel. Foreign direct investment into Israel </span>dropped<span lang="EN-GB"> 46 percent in 2014.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Tens of thousands of students globally have pushed forward the </span>academic boycott<span lang="EN-GB">, backed by figures such as </span>Stephen Hawking<span lang="EN-GB">, </span>Angela Davis<span lang="EN-GB"> and </span>Judith Butler<span lang="EN-GB">. Meanwhile, the likes of author </span>Alice Walker<span lang="EN-GB">, ex-Pink Floyd musician </span>Roger Waters<span lang="EN-GB"> and critically acclaimed filmmaker </span>Ken Loach<span lang="EN-GB"> have lent their support to the </span>cultural boycott<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Importantly, the BDS movement has also been endorsed by </span>anti-colonial Israelis<span lang="EN-GB"> and other </span>Jewish groups<span lang="EN-GB">, as well as </span>Black Lives Matter<span lang="EN-GB">.</span> </p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">Is BDS racist?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Some of Israel's defenders claim that BDS is </span>racist<span lang="EN-GB">. In fact, quite </span>the opposite<span lang="EN-GB"> is true.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Far from targeting individuals on the grounds of ethnicity or nationality, BDS targets institutions on the grounds of complicity in human rights violations and explicitly </span>opposes all forms of racism<span lang="EN-GB">. That's precisely why it seeks to end Israel's entrenched system of </span>racial discrimination and ethnic privilege<span lang="EN-GB">. </span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">In this sense, BDS is comparable with - and takes direct inspiration from - the historic </span>anti-apartheid movement<span lang="EN-GB"> which helped to isolate South Africa globally and </span>end white rule<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">Who opposes BDS?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Since around 2010, Israel has stepped up its </span>fight against BDS<span lang="EN-GB"> and so-called "</span>de-legitimisation"<span lang="EN-GB">. In 2011, the Knesset passed a draconian </span>law<span lang="EN-GB"> against advocating boycotts within Israel. But internationally, this was counterproductive.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">By mid-2015, panicky Israeli leaders were </span>calling<span lang="EN-GB"> the non-violent movement a "strategic threat". Minister of Strategic Affairs Gilad Erdan was </span>given the task of<span lang="EN-GB"> leading the counter-boycott effort, with an annual budget of </span>$25m<span lang="EN-GB"> and supported by </span>military intelligence<span lang="EN-GB"> agencies.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Erdan stresses the need for cooperation with a network of </span>Zionist<span lang="EN-GB"> groups abroad, because </span>ostensibly independent<span lang="EN-GB"> groups are more credible messengers in civil society. He </span>explained<span lang="EN-GB">: "It's not necessarily good that the government is at the front of this battle."</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Because of this, Israeli embassies - many of which have </span>dedicated<span lang="EN-GB"> anti-BDS staff - work closely with </span>Israel lobby<span lang="EN-GB"> groups behind the scenes, as </span>Al Jazeera's investigation<span lang="EN-GB"> "The Lobby" shows. The investigation also confirms another official tactic: establishing </span>front groups<span lang="EN-GB"> and bodies which appear to be </span>grassroots<span lang="EN-GB"> but are actually "</span>astroturf"<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Wealthy supporters of Israel such as </span>Sheldon Adelson<span lang="EN-GB"> and </span>Haim Saban <span lang="EN-GB">in the United States and </span>Trevor Pears<span lang="EN-GB"> in the UK have pumped large sums of money into a plethora of anti-boycott initiatives. And increasingly, the Israeli government and pro-Israel groups </span>team up<span lang="EN-GB"> to create global </span>public-private partnerships<span lang="EN-GB"> against BDS.</span></p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">What has the counter-BDS movement achieved?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Unable to win the argument politically, Israel and its allies are waging an aggressive "</span>lawfare"<span lang="EN-GB"> campaign.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Thanks to friends in high places, they have had considerable success promoting laws to criminalise BDS, with </span>about 20 US states<span lang="EN-GB"> considering anti-boycott bills. Another dimension has been prosecutions of activists, everywhere from </span>Australia<span lang="EN-GB"> to </span>France<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">However, BDS </span>continues<span lang="EN-GB"> to grow rapidly. Legal experts and the European Union have defended the </span>right to boycott<span lang="EN-GB"> as a </span>free speech issue<span lang="EN-GB">. And </span>several<span lang="EN-GB"> pro-Israel lawfare cases - for example in the UK where </span>attempts to suppress BDS<span lang="EN-GB"> are also facing a </span>legal challenge<span lang="EN-GB"> - have </span>failed spectacularly<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></p>
<h2><span lang="EN-GB">What does the future hold?</span></h2>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">Can a voluntary grassroots movement facing off against a well-funded, state-led, and </span>elite-driven<span lang="EN-GB"> counterattack really hope to survive?  </span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB"><span lang="EN-GB">Expect to see the fight get even nastier with more lawsuits, </span>McCarthyism<span lang="EN-GB">, "</span>black ops"<span lang="EN-GB"> and </span>smear tactics<span lang="EN-GB">, covert </span>intelligence gathering<span lang="EN-GB"> and restrictions on BDS activists' </span>freedom<span lang="EN-GB"> of </span>movement<span lang="EN-GB">. As Amnesty International has noted with concern, there have even calls for "</span>targeted civil eliminations"<span lang="EN-GB">.</span></span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">But Israel's forte has always been the hard power of military coercion. Indeed, its diplomats appear unable to comprehend the BDS movement without comparing it with a </span>war<span lang="EN-GB">. It is </span>clueless<span lang="EN-GB"> about how to deal with a non-hierarchical social movement waging a struggle on the terrain of moral persuasion.</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB">As long as Israel flouts international law, no amount of money, repression or "</span>Brand Israel" propaganda<span lang="EN-GB"> will stop the BDS movement continuing to grow and make crucial contributions to Palestinians' struggle for justice.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 3em;"><span style="color: #8e8e8e !important;">Source:</span> <span style="color: #212121 !important;">Al Jazeera</span></p>
