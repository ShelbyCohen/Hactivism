<blockquote><p>Their biggest fear is a repeat of what happened to Occupy earlier this decade, when black bloc violence chased away nonviolent, mainstream protesters — and helped lead to Occupy’s collapse about five years ago.</p>
<p>“That stuff that happened at UC Berkeley with the black bloc was just nuts, doesn’t help,” said longtime protester Buck Bagot, who helped organize the Occupy movement in San Francisco and has been mobilizing demonstrations against Trump. “People need to make sure this incredibly negative, destructive element isn’t there in what we do this time.”</p>
<p>Its adherents saw Occupy as a wake-up call against income inequality. But many of them say Occupy fell short of being more than that, partly because violent anarchists came to be the bandanna-obscured face of the movement to the broader public.</p></blockquote>
<p>The Occupy Wall Street and Occupy protests took over public spaces for several weeks, throughout the country and abroad. Activists &#8212; and not just &#8220;Black bloc&#8221; members &#8212; often sought confrontations with police. Some did so as a <a href="http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2011/10/27/learning-from-hamas-how-occupy-uses-human-shields-veterans-women-the-young-the-old-the-disabled/">deliberate tactic</a> to gain public attention and sympathy through police overreaction.</p>
<p>The encampments, which excluded law enforcement, soon became rife with lawlessness, including a pattern of <a href="http://www.breitbart.com/big-journalism/2011/10/28/updated-occupywallstreet-the-rap-sheet-so-far/">sexual assaults</a>. While most protesters were peaceful, others were not, and the violent contingent went far beyond the &#8220;Black bloc.&#8221;</p>

<div class="ad adinbody if-mobile"><div class="inbodyad" id="mobile-ROS-body1"></div></div>


<p>President Barack Obama and his party supported the Occupy movement, and the media was broadly sympathetic. Violence and lawlessness turned public opinion decisively against the protests, which also proved unsustainable as the onset of winter made camping outside intolerable in most cities.</p>
